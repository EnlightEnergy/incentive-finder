6) Lead capture that feels like a concierge, not a trap
	•	Keep the form short (Name, Work Email, Company, Phone optional, Notes).
	•	Put one compact form in the hero and another contextual form on every Program Detail page pre-filled with that program’s name.
	•	Microcopy under the submit button: “We’ll send your customized incentive summary. No spam.”
	•	Success state: Replace the form with a green confirmation card and a link to schedule an audit.

7) Trust signals that do heavy lifting
	•	Logo bar: SCE, PG&E, SoCalREN, MCE, etc. (lowsat grayscale; color on hover if you want flair).
	•	Client strip: 4–6 recognizables (Fender, Sierra Aluminum…). Keep it tasteful, same height.
	•	One-liner proof: “$X in incentives secured YTD” or “Avg payback: 14 months across 127 sites.”

8) Insights page = insider value (and SEO moat)
	•	Use card teasers: Title, 2-line summary, 3-min read tag.
	•	Topics: Fluorescent ban timing, 0% on-bill finance primer, Custom M&V in manufacturing.
	•	Link Insights where relevant from program cards (“Learn how on-bill works →”).

9) Footer that says “we’re real people”
	•	Left: Enlighting summary line.
	•	Right: Quick links + a compact “Get My Incentive Report” inline form.
	•	Bottom bar: ©, light gray, tiny.

10) Accessibility & mobile polish (non-negotiable)
	•	Touch targets ≥ 44px height.
	•	Focus styles visible on all interactive elements.
	•	Color contrast: buttons/text pass WCAG AA.
	•	Mobile: collapse filters into an accordion; CTA button fixed at bottom on program detail pages for thumb reach.
